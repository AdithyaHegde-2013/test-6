Evolution of Integers

Early Counting Systems

Egyptians, Babylonians, and Sumerians had rich numeral systems, though negatives weren't yet used.

Mayan numerals illustrating early positional ideas.

India's Breakthrough

Indian mathematicians introduced zero and rules for operations with negatives; Brahmagupta (circa 628 CE) formalized them.

Brahmagupta's work clarified integer operations.

China's Negative Numbers

Ancient Chinese used counting rods: red for positive, black for negative ‚Äî early evidence of negative numbers.

Counting tools and rod calculus tradition.

Modern Mathematics

Integers power algebra, coordinates, programming, banking, cryptography, games, and scientific computation.
